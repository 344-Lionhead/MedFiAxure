PROTOCOL

 

• Explain that this is “a usability study for our prototype for a degree planning interface to be integrated into UBC SSC course schedule.”

• Remind participant: “You may drop out from this evaluation whenever you want. We will be conducting a direct observation while giving you tasks to complete to test our interface. As well, we will give you a questionnaire after you complete the walkthrough.”

• Instruct participant to perform the following in order. All tasks are timed unless stated otherwise. Ask participants to think aloud while they are doings this

• Task

• Pretend you’re a 2nd year Computer Science major currently trying to plan courses up until graduation.

• You’ve completed or is in the process of completing courses displayed in the Planner but you still have requirements remaining to be fulfilled

• Try to plan the courses like you would for your actual degree

• Fulfill the following listed requirements

 

1. Ask  to add the remaining 200 level math requirement to planner

 

2. Ask to add 400 level course from requirements - specifically add CPSC 410 to the planner

 

3. Do they notice the prerequisite highlighted

      • Note whether they placed CPSC 410 in the correct term

 

4. Ask how many Arts credits they have remaining to complete

      • did the get it correctly?

 

5. Ask to add Arts course to fulfill remaining arts requirements

      • Did they use the search bar

      • How long it took them to realize to click ARTXXX

 

6. Ask “Pretend you want to take CPSC 404 in the future” ask them to “add it to the plan”

      • Do they notice CPSC 304 also appears?

      • Do they Drag CSPC 304 before CPSC 404?

         If not, Do they immediately realize why they can’t drag CPSC 404?

      • How long does this step takes, also note behaviour

 

7. Ask them to switch their degree, specifically make a new plan for a Major in Computer Engineering and Minor in English

      • Note any struggles

      • The time it takes for them to completed this task

 

8. Thank them for their time. Usability test is over :)
